One project, one screen at a time.
The work is the surface.

Atrium has two tiers. The Overview tells you where the project is. The Active Work tells you what you're doing right now. They never compete for attention — and the loop between them is a single, deliberate motion.

Tier 1 · Overview

Where am I across the project?

One horizontal pipeline. Stages with breathing room. Waves as cards. Loop-backs shown by a quiet chip on the card and a single line of return-story below — never as crossing wires.

Tier 2 · Active Work

What am I doing right now?

Click a wave, the canvas zooms in. The stage's artifact takes the whole screen — spec, mockup, todo stream, Question card. Right rail collapses to icons. Only one thing pulses.

MCP marketplace

2026-05-04 · 6 tickets · Owner · you

Why now

Atrium currently inherits the user's installed MCP servers. To be product-friendly, we want a discoverable marketplace inside the app — installs in two clicks, surfaces capability before commitment.

Acceptance criteria

  • ✓ List installed MCP servers and their tools, grouped by server. STO-2138
  • ✓ Show tool count, last activity, and "in use by N skills" for each server. STO-2139
  • Browse a curated registry of community MCPs — read-only catalogue, no auto-install. STO-2140
  • One-tap install flow that writes to the user's ~/.claude/mcp.json with explicit confirmation. STO-2141
  • Per-server permission preview · which Bash, Edit, and Read paths the MCP can touch. STO-2142
  • Telemetry off by default; explicit toggle for usage metrics. STO-2143

Out of scope

Publishing flow for community MCPs · server-side ratings or reviews · payment-gated MCPs.

Design principles

A reminder of what this is, and what it isn't.
Calm

One thing pulses at a time.

Movement signals where Claude is. Everywhere else, the surface is still. The product breathes; it doesn't fidget.

Two tiers

Where am I, vs what am I doing.

Overview answers the first. Active Work answers the second. They never crowd each other on one screen.

Loops, not wires

The story replaces the diagram.

A chip on the card. A line of return-story. A passing ribbon. Loops are honoured by being told well — not drawn loud.
