Wave 4 · MCP marketplace

Drafted by /requirements · 2026-05-04 · 6 tickets · Owner: you

Why now

Atrium currently inherits the user's installed MCP servers. To be product-friendly, we want a discoverable marketplace inside the app — installs in two clicks, surfaces capability before commitment.

Acceptance criteria

  • ✓List installed MCP servers and their tools, grouped by server.
  • ✓Show tool count, last activity, and "in use by [N skills]" for each server.
  • Browse a curated registry of community MCPs (read-only catalogue, no auto-install).
  • One-tap install flow that writes to the user's ~/.claude/mcp.json with confirmation.
  • Per-server permission preview: which Bash/Edit/Read paths the MCP can touch.
  • Telemetry off by default; explicit toggle for usage metrics.

Out of scope

Publishing flow for community MCPs · server-side ratings/reviews · payment-gated MCPs.

08

Design principles

A reminder of what this is, and what it isn't.
Calm, not flashy

Movement signals state — wave dropping, todo ticking, question arriving. Never decorative motion. The product breathes; it doesn't fidget.

For product people

No jq, no JSONL, no permission JSON. Those live in the drawer. The surface speaks in waves, stages, artifacts, findings, decisions.

Loops are visible

Most tools hide round-trips. Atrium draws them. The arc, the pass badge, the linked findings — every loop has a story you can replay.
